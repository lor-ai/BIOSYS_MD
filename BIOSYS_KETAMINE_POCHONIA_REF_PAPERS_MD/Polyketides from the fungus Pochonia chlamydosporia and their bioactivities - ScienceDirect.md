Abstract

Three previously undescribed griseofulvin derivatives, namely pochonichlamydins A–C, one small

polyketide, namely pochonichlamydin D, together with nine known compounds, have been isolated

from cultures of the fungus Pochonia chlamydosporia. Their structures with absolute configurations

were elucidated on the basis of extensive spectrometric methods and single-crystal X-ray

diffraction. Dechlorogriseofulvin and griseofulvin exhibited inhibitory activities against Candida

albicans at the concentration of 100 μM, with inhibition rates of 69.1% and 56.3%, respectively.

Introduction

Natural products have always been an important source for drug discovery (Newman and Cragg,

2020). Fungal polyketides are a large group of natural products, many of them have been developed

into drugs for the treatment of many diseases. For instance, lovastatin is considered as a potent

cardiovascular drug in cholesterol-lowering (Jiang et al., 2019). Penicillin, discovered from

Penicillium spp., was the golden generation of antibiotics, ushering in a new era in the use of

antibiotics to treat disease (Kardos and Demain, 2011).

Pochonia chlamydosporia (Goddard) Zare & Gams (Clavicipitaceae) is a facultative parasite and

rhizosphere-colonizing fungus (Qin et al., 2019). Previous chemical investigations on this fungus

demonstrated highly productive for resorcylic acid lactones, pyranones, alkaloids and phenolics.

Among them, the famous hallucinogen, ketamine, was also obtained from this fungus (Ferreira et

al., 2020; Ona et al., 2022). Such metabolites have been found to possess antibacterial, anti-malarial,

antivirus, and antitumor activities (Qin et al., 2019; Shen et al., 2015). As part of our ongoing

research on fungal bioactive natural products, the chemical constituents of P. chlamydosporia were

studied. As a result, three previously undescribed griseofulvin derivatives, namely

pochonichlamydins A–C (1–3), one simple polyketide, namely pochonichlamydin D (8), together

with nine known polyketides were isolated from the cultural broth in rice medium (Fig. 1). Their

structures with absolute configurations were elucidated on the basis of extensive spectrometric

methods and single-crystal X-ray crystallographic analysis. All compounds were evaluated for their

Conclusion

Thirteen polyketides including four previously undescribed ones were isolated from the fungus P.

chlamydosporia, which were comprised of griseofulvin derivatives, spirocyclic alkaloids, resorcylic

acid lactones, and small polyketides. Griseofulvin and its analogues have been obtained from P.

chlamydosporia for the first time. Compounds 6 and 7 showed inhibitory activities against Candida

albicans, and compound 3 exhibited mild cytotoxicity against the human cancer MCF-7 cell line.
