3. Synthesis method

3.1. One-step method

6. Conclusion

Sulfonated polyimides (SPIs), one of the classes of high-performance polymers exhibit excellent

thermal stability, mechanical properties, and ion exchange capacities, making them suitable to use

in membrane application such as in desalination, fuel cell or in wastewater industry. However, SPI

thin films are hindered by drawbacks such as swelling behavior and degradation associated with

their sulfonation degree. Thus, this review focuses on investigating the characterization, thermal

and mechanical properties of SPI based on various combinations of sulfonated polyimide isomer.

Flexible substituent groups such as ether linkages or alkyl chains offer promise in enhancing

mechanical stability by adding flexibility to the polyimide backbone. SPIs with rigid substituent

groups such as Trifluoromethyl (-CF ) or triphenylphosphine oxide moieties have higher swelling
